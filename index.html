HAZARA PEOEPLE CULUTRE

The Hazaras (Persian: هزاره, romanized: həzārə; Hazaragi: آزره, romanized: āzrə) are an ethnic group and a principal component of the population of Afghanistan, native to, and primarily residing in the Hazaristan region in central Afghanistan and the northern regions of the Baluchistan province in Pakistan.[23] They are one of the largest ethnic groups in Afghanistan, [24][25][26][27] and a significant minority group in Pakistan, mostly in Quetta,[28][5] and as well as in Iran.[6] They speak the Dari and Hazaragi dialects of Persian. Dari, one of the two official languages in Afghanistan. [29][30][31][32][33]
